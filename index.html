Keeping up with their tradition since last two years Mars Rover Manipal was the first team to submit the SAR. MRM is one of the five teams from India to be selected, along with teams from IIT Bombay, VIT Vellore, SRM University, and IIT Madras. Mars Rover Manipal is very excited since the declaration of the results and is working even harder. The rover for URC 2018 is named ‘Airawat’. The rover is capable of performing tasks such as autonomous traversal, extreme retrieval, equipment servicing and soil collection and testing amongst others. A team of 20 members have travelled to USA to represent Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE) on the big stage. The team was ranked Asia's best team in URC 2017 with a worldwide position of 8. MRM also won the inaugural Indian Rover Challenge, held in January 2018.

University Rover Challenge is organized by the Mars Society of USA. The competition is scheduled to be held between May 31 and June 2 at Mars Desert Research Station, Hanksville, Utah, USA. The SAR round is a competitive milestone, only after which the teams are invited to the competition site. The package consists of a video and a report, which explains the core rover systems, approach to competition Tasks, testing and operations, and details such as budget and team progress.

Team Mars Rover Manipal, powered by Mouser Electronics and Wheelez Inc, which has reached USA , few days ago, has successfully cleared the System Acceptance Review (SAR) round for University Rover Challenge 2018.
